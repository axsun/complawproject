3h.1 Did landlord state just cause for termination of lease?

This means that your landlord has a legally acceptable reason for evicting you which is written in the notice to terminate. Some examples of "just cause" include:

1. Not paying rent;

2. Breaching important lease agreement provisions;

3. Creating a nuisance;

4. Serious property damage;

5. Criminal activities including threats of violence to the landlord;

6. Refusing to sign a lease extension with similar terms as the original;

7. Illegally subletting or assigning rights to others;

8. Preventing the landlord from legally entering for repairs or showing the property;

9. A terminated resident manager refusing to leave;

10. Using the property for illegal purposes; and

11. Failure to move after giving the landlord notice of intent to move.
